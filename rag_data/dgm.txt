PNB Scale VI Deputy General Manager is a senior leadership position within Punjab National Bank.
The Deputy General Manager role involves overseeing large geographical zones or major head office departments.
The position focuses on strategic execution of bank policies and large-scale operational supervision.
A Deputy General Manager monitors regional business performance and ensures alignment with the bank’s long-term strategic goals.
The officer also evaluates high-value credit proposals and ensures implementation of regulatory and internal banking policies across multiple units.

The Deputy General Manager leads zonal operations or supervises major head office functional departments.
The role includes monitoring the performance of multiple regions and managing large banking business portfolios.
The officer evaluates and recommends high-value corporate and consortium credit proposals submitted by regional offices.
The position involves driving recovery strategies for stressed assets and reducing non-performing assets across the zone.
The Deputy General Manager ensures effective implementation of bank-wide initiatives, digital transformation programs, and regulatory directives.
The officer also coordinates with Executive Directors and head office leadership for strategic planning and business development.

The Deputy General Manager has authority to approve high-value credit proposals within delegated powers defined by the bank.
The officer can recommend very large corporate credit exposures to the General Manager or the Board for final approval.
The role carries responsibility for zone-wide business performance, regulatory compliance, and profitability.

The position requires strong strategic thinking and leadership capability to manage large banking operations.
The officer must have deep expertise in corporate credit evaluation, risk assessment, and portfolio management.
Experience in supervising large-scale banking operations across regions or departments is essential.
The Deputy General Manager must possess the ability to interpret banking policies and regulatory frameworks effectively.
The role also requires strong stakeholder management with corporate clients, regulators, and government bodies.

The next promotion path after Deputy General Manager is General Manager, which is classified as a Scale VII leadership position.
Promotion to the General Manager role usually depends on seniority, performance evaluation, vigilance clearance, and promotion board selection.

The Deputy General Manager generally reports to the General Manager or directly to the Executive Director depending on the bank’s organizational structure.
The officer supervises Assistant General Managers, Chief Managers, Regional Heads, and departmental leadership teams.

Banks provide executive leadership and strategic banking programs to prepare Deputy General Managers for higher leadership roles.
Training programs include advanced corporate credit evaluation and risk portfolio management courses.
Officers also receive regulatory compliance and governance training related to banking supervision.
Additional programs include national-level banking policy workshops and leadership development initiatives.
