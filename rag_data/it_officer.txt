PNB Scale I Officer is the entry-level management position in Punjab National Bank.
The role serves as the starting point for officers entering the bank’s managerial hierarchy.
A Scale I Officer handles customer service activities, routine banking transactions, and branch operational tasks.
The position also involves learning banking systems, operational procedures, and regulatory requirements applicable to the banking sector.
The officer gains experience in retail banking operations while supporting overall branch performance.

The Scale I Officer is responsible for opening customer accounts and completing Know Your Customer verification procedures.
The officer handles customer deposits, withdrawals, and routine banking transactions at the branch.
The role includes responding to customer queries and providing guidance on banking services and products.
A Scale I Officer assists in processing retail loan applications and preparing the required documentation.
The officer also contributes to branch business growth by supporting marketing and customer relationship activities.
The position requires strict compliance with Reserve Bank of India regulations and internal bank policies.

A Scale I Officer can recommend retail loan proposals after performing basic credit assessment and documentation review.
The officer cannot independently approve large credit proposals or high-value loans.
The position operates under the supervision and guidance of the Branch Manager or senior officers.

The role requires a basic understanding of banking regulations and operational guidelines.
The officer must possess strong customer service skills to assist clients and resolve their queries effectively.
Basic credit analysis skills are required for evaluating retail loan applications.
The position also requires the ability to manage banking systems and maintain accurate documentation.

The next promotion path after Scale I Officer is Manager, which is classified as a Scale II position.
The typical promotion timeline for this role is approximately three to five years depending on performance, internal examinations, and promotion policies.

A Scale I Officer usually reports to the Branch Manager, who may be a Scale II or Scale III officer depending on the branch structure.
The officer may supervise clerical staff or support staff when responsibilities are delegated.

Banks provide induction training programs to prepare Scale I Officers for banking operations and professional responsibilities.
Training programs include core banking system training and operational banking modules.
Officers also receive training in retail banking products, regulatory compliance, and customer service practices.
