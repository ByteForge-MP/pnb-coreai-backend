PNB Scale IV Chief Manager is a senior managerial position within Punjab National Bank.
The role is responsible for managing large branches or overseeing specialized banking departments.
A Chief Manager supervises multiple operational teams and ensures efficient execution of banking functions.
The position involves managing significant credit exposure while maintaining regulatory compliance and business profitability.

The Chief Manager leads large branches or specialized functional departments within the bank.
The role includes supervising Senior Managers, Managers, and operational teams responsible for daily banking activities.
The officer reviews and approves higher-value credit proposals within the delegated financial authority of the bank.
The position requires monitoring branch profitability and ensuring effective control of non-performing assets.
The Chief Manager is also responsible for implementing recovery strategies for stressed loan accounts.
The officer ensures that bank policies, compliance requirements, and strategic initiatives are implemented effectively.
The role requires coordination with regional offices and zonal offices for business planning and performance monitoring.

The Chief Manager has authority to approve substantial credit proposals within delegated limits defined by the bank.
The officer may recommend high-value corporate loan proposals to higher authorities for final approval.
The position carries responsibility for achieving business growth targets and maintaining operational risk control within the branch or department.

The role requires strong expertise in credit risk evaluation and financial analysis.
The Chief Manager must possess strategic planning skills to support business development and operational efficiency.
Leadership capability is essential to manage multiple teams and ensure coordinated branch operations.
The officer must also ensure adherence to audit requirements and regulatory compliance standards.
The position requires the ability to manage relationships with corporate clients and important banking customers.

The next promotion path after Chief Manager is Assistant General Manager, which is classified as a Scale V leadership position.
The typical promotion timeline for this role is approximately three to five years depending on performance and leadership evaluation.
Promotion decisions are generally based on performance records, leadership capability, and the bank’s promotion process.

The Chief Manager usually reports to the Regional Head or the Zonal Office depending on the bank’s organizational structure.
The officer supervises Senior Managers, Managers, and operational teams responsible for branch or departmental operations.

Banks provide advanced corporate credit training programs to prepare Chief Managers for higher responsibilities.
Training programs also include leadership development and strategic management workshops.
Officers receive training in risk management practices and regulatory compliance frameworks.
