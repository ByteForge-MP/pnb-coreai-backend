Assistant General Manager, also known as a Scale V Officer, is a senior leadership role in the banking hierarchy.
This position is responsible for overseeing multiple branches and managing regional banking operations.
The role involves monitoring regional business performance across deposits, advances, and profitability.
An Assistant General Manager evaluates large credit proposals and supports strategic banking initiatives.
The officer ensures compliance with regulatory requirements and internal banking policies.

The Assistant General Manager supervises the performance of multiple branches or functional departments within the assigned region.
The role includes monitoring business growth across deposits, advances, and overall profitability.
The officer reviews large credit proposals submitted by branches and recommends them for approval when required.
The role also involves ensuring effective management of non-performing assets and implementing recovery strategies.
The officer is responsible for implementing bank policies, digital banking initiatives, and operational improvement programs.
The Assistant General Manager coordinates with the zonal office and head office for effective strategic execution.

The Assistant General Manager has authority to approve large credit limits within the delegated financial powers assigned by the bank.
The officer can recommend major corporate credit proposals to higher authorities for final approval.
The role carries responsibility for achieving regional business targets and maintaining operational efficiency across branches.

The position requires strong expertise in corporate credit analysis and risk assessment.
The officer must possess strategic leadership skills and the ability to plan regional business growth.
Decision-making must be aligned with banking regulations and internal policy frameworks.
The role requires strong team leadership to guide branch managers and departmental teams.
The officer must also maintain strong relationships with corporate clients and other stakeholders.

The next promotion path after Assistant General Manager is Deputy General Manager, which is classified as a Scale VI position.
The typical promotion timeline for this role is approximately three to four years depending on performance and leadership evaluation.
Promotion decisions are based on performance records, leadership capabilities, and promotion board assessments.

The Assistant General Manager typically reports to the Zonal Office or directly to the General Manager depending on the organizational structure.
The officer supervises Chief Managers, Senior Managers, and branch leadership teams within the assigned region.

Banks provide executive leadership development programs to prepare Assistant General Managers for higher leadership responsibilities.
Training programs include corporate credit evaluation and project finance management.
Officers also receive training in strategic banking management and risk management practices.
Additional training programs focus on advanced recovery strategies and stressed asset resolution.
