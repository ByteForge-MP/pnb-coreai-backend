TOPIC: PNB SCALE VII GENERAL MANAGER POSITION

SECTION: Role Overview
Scale VII General Manager is a top executive position responsible for leading large banking circles, national business verticals, or key head office divisions. The GM drives strategic planning, oversees large credit portfolios, manages risk exposure, and ensures the achievement of business and regulatory objectives across multiple zones or departments.

SECTION: Key Responsibilities
Leading large circles, national business verticals, or major head office departments.
Monitoring large-scale credit portfolios, profitability, and asset quality.
Approving or recommending very high-value credit proposals.
Driving bank-wide strategic initiatives and transformation programs.
Ensuring regulatory compliance and governance standards across units.
Representing the bank in industry forums, consortium meetings, and regulatory discussions.

SECTION: Decision Authority
Can approve very large credit exposures within delegated authority.
Can recommend mega corporate credit proposals to Board level.
Responsible for circle-level profitability, compliance, and strategic execution.

SECTION: Skills Required
Strong strategic leadership and vision.
Expertise in large credit risk and portfolio management.
Policy formulation and governance capability.
National-level stakeholder and corporate relationship management.
Crisis management and decision-making under high responsibility.

SECTION: Promotion Path
Next Position: Scale VIII Chief General Manager.
Promotion Basis: Seniority-cum-merit, performance record, vigilance clearance, and Board approval.

SECTION: Reporting Structure
Reports to: Executive Director / Managing Director & CEO.
Supervises: DGMs, AGMs, Circle Heads, Zonal Heads, and departmental leadership teams.

SECTION: Training Provided
Executive leadership and governance programs.
National banking strategy workshops.
Advanced corporate risk and portfolio management courses.
Board-level policy and regulatory training modules.
