TOPIC: PNB SCALE VIII CHIEF GENERAL MANAGER POSITION

SECTION: Role Overview
Scale VIII Chief General Manager is an apex executive leadership position responsible for overseeing national business verticals, major circles, or strategic head office functions. The CGM contributes to policy execution, large-scale credit oversight, governance, and institutional growth while supporting the Executive Directors and the Board in strategic decision-making.

SECTION: Key Responsibilities
Leading major national business verticals or large banking circles.
Supervising high-value corporate credit portfolios and strategic exposures.
Implementing Board-approved policies and long-term banking strategies.
Driving digital transformation, governance improvements, and operational reforms.
Monitoring bank-wide risk, profitability, and compliance indicators.
Representing the bank in high-level regulatory, government, and consortium forums.

SECTION: Decision Authority
Can approve extremely high-value credit proposals within delegated Board powers.
Can recommend mega strategic credit exposures to Executive Director or Board.
Responsible for national-level performance, governance, and policy implementation.

SECTION: Skills Required
Strong institutional leadership and governance capability.
Expertise in large corporate credit, risk, and portfolio supervision.
Strategic policy interpretation and execution ability.
High-level stakeholder management with regulators, corporates, and government.
Vision for organizational growth and transformation.

SECTION: Promotion Path
Next Position: Executive Director.
Promotion Basis: Government approval, Board selection, seniority-cum-merit, and leadership record.

SECTION: Reporting Structure
Reports to: Executive Director / Managing Director & CEO.
Supervises: General Managers, DGMs, vertical heads, and national program teams.

SECTION: Training Provided
Board-level governance and policy programs.
National financial leadership workshops.
Strategic banking and institutional management courses.
Regulatory and international banking exposure programs.
