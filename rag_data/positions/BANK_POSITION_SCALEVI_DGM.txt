TOPIC: PNB SCALE VI DEPUTY GENERAL MANAGER POSITION

SECTION: Role Overview
Scale VI Deputy General Manager is a high-level leadership role responsible for overseeing large geographical zones or major head office departments. The DGM drives strategic execution, supervises regional performance, evaluates high-value credit proposals, and ensures the implementation of bank policies across multiple units.

SECTION: Key Responsibilities
Leading zonal operations or major head office functions.
Monitoring performance of multiple regions and large business portfolios.
Evaluating and recommending high-value corporate and consortium credit proposals.
Driving recovery strategies for stressed assets and NPA reduction.
Implementing bank-wide initiatives, digital programs, and regulatory directives.
Coordinating with Executive Directors and Head Office for strategic planning.

SECTION: Decision Authority
Can approve high-value credit proposals within delegated powers.
Can recommend very large corporate exposures to General Manager or Board level.
Responsible for zone-wide performance, compliance, and profitability.

SECTION: Skills Required
Strong strategic thinking and leadership capability.
Expertise in corporate credit, risk, and portfolio management.
Large-scale operational supervision experience.
Policy implementation and regulatory interpretation ability.
Stakeholder management with corporates and government bodies.

SECTION: Promotion Path
Next Position: Scale VII General Manager.
Typical Promotion Time: Based on seniority, performance, vigilance clearance, and promotion board selection.

SECTION: Reporting Structure
Reports to: General Manager / Executive Director.
Supervises: AGMs, Chief Managers, Regional Heads, and departmental leadership teams.

SECTION: Training Provided
Executive leadership and strategic banking programs.
Advanced corporate credit and risk portfolio management courses.
Regulatory and governance training.
National-level banking policy workshops.
