In Punjab National Bank, promotion from General Manager to Chief General Manager represents advancement from Scale VII to Scale VIII in the bank’s executive leadership hierarchy.
A General Manager must currently be serving in Scale VII to become eligible for promotion to Chief General Manager.

The officer must complete the minimum tenure required for the General Manager position before being considered for promotion.
The officer must also fall within the promotion consideration zone defined by the Human Resources department of Punjab National Bank.
The promotion candidate must have a clean vigilance record and must receive integrity clearance before being considered for elevation.

Punjab National Bank evaluates the performance of General Managers before promotion to Chief General Manager.
The bank reviews the Annual Performance Appraisal Reports of the last five years for each candidate.
The APAR ratings during this period are generally expected to be Very Good or above.

The bank evaluates the business performance of the circle or national vertical managed by the General Manager.
The promotion committee reviews the officer’s record in controlling non-performing assets and improving recovery performance.
Profitability and business growth achieved under the leadership of the General Manager are also considered during evaluation.
The bank also assesses the officer’s contribution to strategic initiatives and transformation programs.

Punjab National Bank prefers General Managers who have experience handling large banking circles or national business verticals.
Experience in supervising large corporate credit portfolios and high-value financial exposures is considered important.
Experience in head office policy roles or strategic planning functions is also valued.
The ability to represent the bank in regulatory forums, consortium banking meetings, and institutional discussions is considered a leadership advantage.

The promotion process begins when the Human Resources department prepares a list of eligible General Managers based on seniority and service records.
A promotion committee evaluates the candidates based on performance, integrity, leadership capability, and institutional contribution.

Shortlisted candidates may be recommended for further consideration by the Board of Directors.
Final approval for promotion from General Manager to Chief General Manager is granted by the Board of Directors.

Since Punjab National Bank is a public sector bank, promotion to Chief General Manager also requires approval from the Government of India.

After promotion, the selected officers are elevated to the Scale VIII Chief General Manager position.
Chief General Managers are typically assigned responsibility for major banking circles, national business verticals, or strategic head office departments.
